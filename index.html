Rave Safety Guide

No judgment. Just look out for each other.

Ravers Unite does not encourage drug use. But if people are going to party, the app should help them recognize risky situations, stay with their crew, and get help fast when something feels wrong.

Emergency

If someone seems in trouble

Get venue medical staff or security right away, stay with them, and contact emergency services if the situation feels serious.

Buddy system

Do not let people disappear alone

Arrive with a check-in plan, pick a meetup spot, and use solo buddy requests before the event starts.

Hydration

Drink water steadily

Take breaks, cool down, use electrolytes when needed, and avoid overdoing water too quickly.

Rest

Sleep before the long night

Going into a bender exhausted makes everything harder. Rest before, eat something, and plan recovery time after.

Harm reduction

Know what resources exist

Where legal and available, fentanyl test strips and naloxone can reduce risk. Learn where local harm-reduction supplies are before the event.

Red flags

When in doubt, get help

Confusion, overheating, collapse, trouble breathing, blue or gray lips, or not waking up are reasons to get medical help immediately.
